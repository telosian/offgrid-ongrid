# offgrid-ongrid

A practical guide to emergency food independence — staying fed when the grid goes down, even if you never leave the house.

## How Much Does One Person Need?

Plan for roughly **2,000 calories per day**. Here's what that breaks down to:

| Nutrient | Daily Need |
|----------|------------|
| Calories | 2,000 kcal |
| Protein  | 50 g       |
| Carbs    | 250 g      |
| Fat      | 65 g       |
| Fiber    | 25 g       |
| Water    | 3 L (¾ gal)|

A day of eating on stored food looks something like this:

| Meal | Food | Amount | Cal | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|------|------|--------|-----|---------|-------|-----|
| Breakfast | Oats | 1 cup dry (80g) | 300 | 10g | 54g | 5g |
| | Peanut butter | 2 tbsp (32g) | 190 | 7g | 7g | 16g |
| | Powdered milk | 3 tbsp (24g) | 80 | 8g | 12g | 0g |
| Lunch | Rice | 1 cup dry (185g) | 675 | 13g | 148g | 1g |
| | Beans (dried, cooked) | 1 cup cooked (170g) | 245 | 15g | 45g | 1g |
| | Vegetable oil | 1 tbsp | 120 | 0g | 0g | 14g |
| Dinner | Pasta | 2 oz dry (56g) | 200 | 7g | 42g | 1g |
| | Canned tuna | 1 can (140g) | 150 | 33g | 0g | 1g |
| | Vegetable oil | 1 tbsp | 120 | 0g | 0g | 14g |
| Snack | Dried fruit | ¼ cup (40g) | 100 | 1g | 26g | 0g |
| **Total** | | | **~2,180** | **~94g** | **~334g** | **~53g** |

Add bran cereal or extra beans to hit fiber goals. Add more oil/peanut butter if you need more fat calories.
