# Risks & Mitigations

## Home

### Fire

**Before:**
- Install smoke/CO detectors on every floor, test monthly
- Keep fire extinguisher in kitchen and garage
- Store important docs and HDDs in a fireproof bag or grab-bag near the door
- Know two exits from every room
- Keep brush/vegetation cleared around the house

**During:**
- Get everyone out — don't go back for stuff
- Stay low under smoke
- Call 911 once outside
- Meet at a pre-agreed spot

**After:**
- Don't re-enter until cleared by fire dept
- Document damage with photos for insurance
- Contact insurance ASAP
- Arrange temporary shelter

---

### Flood

**Before:**
- Know your flood zone
- Keep sandbags or flood barriers on hand
- Store emergency supplies above ground level
- Have a sump pump with battery backup
- Keep important items elevated or in waterproof containers

**During:**
- Move to higher ground — don't walk/drive through floodwater
- Shut off electricity at the breaker if safe to do so
- Avoid contact with floodwater (contamination)

**After:**
- Document damage before cleanup
- Dry out the house ASAP to prevent mold (fans, dehumidifiers)
- Discard food that contacted floodwater
- Check structural integrity before full re-entry

---

### Power Outage (Extended)

**Before:**
- Stock propane stove + fuel (see emergency-supplies.md)
- Have flashlights, batteries, and a hand-crank radio
- Keep phones and battery banks charged
- Freeze water jugs to keep freezer cold longer
- Know how to manually open garage door

**During:**
- Keep fridge/freezer closed as much as possible (good for ~4hr / ~48hr)
- Use propane stove for cooking, never indoors without ventilation
- Conserve phone battery — airplane mode when not in use
- Check on neighbors

**After:**
- Discard perishable food if above 40F for 2+ hours
- Reset clocks, check sump pump, restart any critical systems
- Restock fuel and batteries

---

### Earthquake

**Before:**
- Secure heavy furniture and water heater to walls
- Store shoes and a flashlight near the bed
- Know how to shut off gas
- Keep emergency water and food stocked

**During:**
- Drop, cover, hold on — get under sturdy furniture
- Stay away from windows and heavy objects
- If outdoors, move to open area away from buildings

**After:**
- Check for gas leaks (smell, hissing) — shut off valve if suspected
- Check for structural damage before staying in the house
- Expect aftershocks
- Use texts instead of calls to keep lines open

---

### Severe Storm / Tornado

**Before:**
- Identify a safe room (interior, lowest floor, no windows)
- Have a weather radio or alerts on phone
- Trim dead branches near the house
- Secure outdoor furniture and loose items

**During:**
- Go to safe room immediately
- Stay away from windows
- Cover yourself with blankets/mattress for debris protection

**After:**
- Watch for downed power lines
- Check roof and structure for damage
- Document and photograph everything for insurance

## Water Supply

### Contamination / Loss of Municipal Water

**Before:**
- Store at least 3 days of water (1 gal/person/day minimum, 3L drinking + cooking)
- Keep water purification tablets and a portable filter
- Know nearest natural water source

**During:**
- Use stored water first
- Boil or filter any questionable water before drinking
- Ration if uncertain how long outage will last

**After:**
- Wait for official all-clear before using tap water again
- Flush pipes before resuming normal use
- Restock water supplies

---

## Food Supply

### Supply Chain Disruption

**Before:**
- Maintain 30-day dry food supply (see daily-nutrition.md)
- Rotate stock — use oldest first
- Grow a small garden if possible

**During:**
- Ration based on how long disruption may last
- Prioritize calorie-dense foods (rice, beans, oil, peanut butter)
- Share/trade with neighbors

**After:**
- Restock gradually as supply returns
- Note what you ran low on and adjust future storage

---

## Personal Safety

### Medical Emergency

**Before:**
- Keep a stocked first aid kit
- Take a basic first aid / CPR class
- Know nearest hospital route without GPS
- Keep a list of medications and allergies for each household member

**During:**
- Call 911
- Apply first aid as trained
- Don't move injured person unless in immediate danger

**After:**
- Follow up with medical care
- Restock first aid kit
- Debrief — what could have been prevented

---

## Communications

### Loss of Cell / Internet

**Before:**
- Have a battery-powered or hand-crank AM/FM radio
- Agree on a family meetup point and out-of-area contact person
- Keep a paper list of important phone numbers

**During:**
- Use SMS over calls (works on weaker signal)
- Try Wi-Fi calling if available
- Go to meetup point if unable to communicate

**After:**
- Check in with all family/contacts
- Recharge devices
- Review what communication methods worked
